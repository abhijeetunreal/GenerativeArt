Abstract Visual Exercise in TouchDesigner

This exercise in TouchDesigner focuses on procedural abstraction and motion design, exploring shape manipulation, transformations, and effects to generate dynamic visuals.

Shape Manipulation & Curve Transformation
Understanding how to use Superquad as a base shape.
Converting shapes into Bézier curves for flexible control over form.

Working with Points & Lines
Extracting and modifying point data for generative patterns.
Creating line-based structures to add complexity to the composition.

Applying Noise for Organic Motion
Using Noise TOP/SOP to add randomness and variation.
Enhancing movement and texture through controlled distortion.

Time-Based Animation
Leveraging absTime to drive continuous transformations.
Experimenting with rotation, scaling, and movement over time.

Image Processing & Composition
Using Blur to soften edges and create depth.
Layering different elements through compositing for a refined final output.

Creative Experimentation & Style Development
Exploring abstract forms and motion for unique visual aesthetics.
Developing procedural workflows that can be modified and iterated upon. This exercise builds foundational skills in procedural design, focusing on shape transformation, generative motion, and real-time visual composition. The experience gained here is valuable for abstract animation, digital art, and motion graphics in TouchDesigner.
